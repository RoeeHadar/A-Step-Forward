# Whole Numbers

Whole numbers are the counting numbers together with zero: 0, 1, 2, 3, and so on. We use them whenever we count objects, measure distance in whole steps, or label items in order.

## Why whole numbers matter

If you have three apples, the number **3** tells you how many apples there are. If a shelf holds zero books, **0** is still a meaningful quantity—it means none.

## Try it

Start at 12 and count forward by ones until you reach 20. Then count backward from 20 to 12. Say each number aloud and notice how each step adds or removes one item from your count.

## Check your understanding

Name one situation at home and one at school where you use whole numbers. Explain what each number counts.
